Dr. Vikram Sarabhai

The Father of the Indian Space Programme

Here's a time line of Dr. Vikram Sarabhai's life:


    Early Life

  • 1919 - Birth: Born on August 12 in Ahmedabad, India.

    • Education

  • 1938 - Education: Graduated with a Bachelor’s degree in Physics from the University of Cambridge, England.
  • 1983 - Completed his B.Sc. in Physics from Gujarat College, Ahmedabad.
  • 1940 - Studied at the University of Cambridge, earning a degree in Natural Sciences.
  • 1947 - Completed his Ph.D. at the Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore.

    • Career and Contributions

  • 1947 - Returned to India and began working in the field of physics and electronics.
  • 1962 - Established the Indian National Committee for Space Research (INCOSPAR).
  • 1963 - Founded the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and became its first chairman.
  • 1965 - Instrumental in the establishment of the Indian Institute of Management (IIM) in Ahmedabad.
  • 1969 - Played a key role in setting up the Space Applications Centre in Ahmedabad.

    • Achievements and Honors

  • 1970 - Awarded the Padma Bhushan, India’s third-highest civilian award.
  • 1972 - Received the International Academy of Astronautics’ (IAA) award.

    • Later Life and Legacy

  • 1971 - Chaired the planning committee for the Indian National Programme on Space Applications.
  • 1973 - Died on December 30 in a tragic incident in Kerala, India.

    • Posthumous Recognition

  • 1975 - ISRO launched the first Indian satellite, Aryabhata, in his memory.
  • 2002 - The Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre in Thiruvananthapuram was named in his honor.

"The development of science and technology in India is in many ways the result of the vision and effort of Vikram Sarabhai."

-- President of India Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam
